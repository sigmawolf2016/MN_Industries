Rainbow Six Seige

Rainbow Six Siege, developed by Ubisoft, is a tactical first-person shooter that emphasizes strategy, teamwork, and destructible environments. Released in December 2015, the game pits two teams of five players against each other, where one team defends and the other attacks. The defending team fortifies a location with barricades, traps, and reinforcements, while the attacking team uses a range of specialized operators and gadgets to breach and secure the objective. Siege's unique blend of strategy, realism, and high-stakes gameplay, combined with its evolving roster of operators and maps, has made it a standout title in the tactical shooter genre.

Valorant

Valorant is a popular tactical first-person shooter developed by Riot Games, blending precise gunplay with unique character abilities. Released in June 2020, it features a roster of Agents, each with their own special skills that add layers of strategy to the traditional shooter formula. Set in a futuristic world, players engage in 5v5 matches where one team attacks and the other defends, with the primary objective often revolving around planting or defusing a spike.
Valorant’s combination of strategic depth, fast-paced action, and vibrant art style has garnered a dedicated following and solidified its place in the competitive gaming scene.

Roblox

Roblox is an online platform that allows users to create, share, and play games and experiences created by other users. Launched in 2006, it offers a vast and diverse array of user-generated content across genres, from action and adventure to simulation and role-playing. What sets Roblox apart is its intuitive game development tools, which empower players of all ages to design their own games and virtual worlds using the platform’s scripting language, Lua. With a strong focus on community and creativity, Roblox has become a global phenomenon, especially popular among younger audiences, and continues to grow with frequent updates and an expanding library of user-generated content.
